Global Data on Sustainable Energy (2000 - 2019)

The transition to sustainable energy sources is one of the most critical challenges facing our planet in the 21st century. As we confront the pressing issues of climate change and environmental sustainability, it becomes increasingly vital to analyze and understand the trends in sustainable energy adoption across the globe. This data visualization offers a comprehensive overview of how nations worldwide have progressed in adopting clean and renewable energy sources from 2000 to 2019.

The visualized data will not only provide an overall picture but also offer continental insights, highlighting how North America, South America, Europe, Africa, Asia, and Oceania have navigated the challenges of sustainable energy adoption differently.

It focuses on key metrics, such as access to electricity, renewable energy capacity, carbon emissions, and economic growth. By examining these metrics, we can identify patterns and correlations that help us make informed decisions for a more sustainable and energy-efficient future.

Energy Consumption per Person in kWh vs. GDP per capita

This interactive bubble chart visualizes the relationship between energy consumption per person in kilowatt-hours (kWh) and GDP per capita for countries around the world. The chart provides insights into the energy efficiency and economic prosperity of different nations over the years from 2000 to 2019.

  • You can select a specific year within the range of 2000 to 2019 to see how energy consumption and GDP per capita have evolved over time by using the slider.
  • You can also use the slider to filter countries based on their population density. It enables you to explore the data for countries with a minimum population density threshold.
  • The dropdown allows you to filter countries by continent, making it easy to focus on specific regions of interest.
  • Different colours represents each continents and different size of the bubble shows the size of the population density.

South America and Africa tends to have lower GDP per capita and energy consumption over the 20 years compared to other continents. Some of the European countries have higher GDP per capita than all the other countries in the world and especially Luxembourg has the highest GDP per capita from 2000 to 2019.

Access to electricity (% of population) Distribution 2000-2019

This interative choropleth map provides a visual representation of access to electricity as a percentage of the population for countries around the world from 2000 to 2019. The chart offers insights into the disparities and trends in electricity accessibility, highlighting regions with varying levels of infrastructure development.

  • You can select a specific year within the range of 2000 to 2019 to explore the changes in access to electricity over time by using the slider.
  • Changes in the colour shows the different level of the value which is percentage of population in this choropleth map.

Most of the countries in Asia, Oceania, Europe, North America and South America has very high percentage of population with access to electricity throughout the years. Compared to other continents, Africa has low percentage of population. However, over the years, the percentage increased and in 2019, most of the countries in Africa reaches about 60% or more.

Total CO2 Emissions in Each Continents 2000-2019

This interactive donut chart provides a comprehensive overview of total carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions for different continents from the year 2000 to 2019. It offers a visual representation of how emissions are distributed across continents during this time period.

  • You can use the slider to select a specific year within the range of 2000 to 2019 to explore the changes in CO2 emissions by continent over time.
  • Different colours represents each continents.

The most proportion of total CO2 emission is from Asia and Oceania has the lowest total emission over the years. North America and Europe have a decreasing trend in the proportion of total emission over the years and Asia has an increasing trend. Total CO2 emission of all the other continents except Europe and North America has increased from 2000 to 2019. However, the toal emission of Europe and North America fluctuated in between but comparing 2000 and 2019, the total emission decreased.

Percentage of Electricity from Low Carbon Sources 2000-2019

This piechart illustrates the changing landscape of electricity generation across continents from the year 2000 to 2019. It provides valuable insights into the adoption of low-carbon energy sources by different regions around the world.

  • You can use the year slider to select a specific year within the range of 2000 to 2019 and observe how the percentage of electricity generated from low-carbon sources varies across continents during that period.
  • Different colours represents each continents.

Africa and Europe has high percentage of electricity from low-carbon sources throughout the years and Oceania has the lowest percentage compared to other continents. The percentage of Europe increased from 2000 to 2019 where the percentage of Africa has decreased. The percentage of electricity from low-carbon sources of other continents does not change much and remains almost the same.

Different Sources of Electricity by Continent

This stacked bar chart offers a comprehensive view of the sources of electricity generation across continents. By categorizing energy sources and aggregating their production, it provides an insightful representation of the global energy landscape over the years.

  • The dropdown allows you to select a specific continent or choose to view data for all continents combined.
  • Different sources of electricity is represented with different colours as shown in the legend.

In general, most of the electricity is from fossil fuels and nuclear energy has the lowest proportion of the total. Also, there is an increasing trend in the amount of generated electricity in total. Europe and North America have relatively high proportion of electricity generated from nuclear energy compared to other continents.

Percentage of Renewable Energy in Final Energy Consumption

This choropleth map visualizes the percentage of renewable energy in the total final energy consumption across the world. By using a color-coded scheme and geographical representation, it provides an informative view of the global renewable energy landscape.

  • The slider allows you to select a specific year within the range of 2000 to 2019 and changes in the colour shows the different percentage.

Throughout the years, for all the continents, the percentage of renewable eneregy in final energy consumption has decreased. As opposed to the access to electricity map, many countries in Africa have high percentage that is over 60% in general and the rest of the countries have low percentage which is about 40% or below.
